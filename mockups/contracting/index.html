Contracting Hub — three layout mockups

Same feature, three layouts. Each one tracks carrier contracting requests (submitted / approved / denied) and writing numbers for you and your downline, alerts a downline when an upline gets approved so they know they can now contract, and supports requesting a different upline when your normal upline is blocked. Open each, click around, then tell me which direction feels right.

A · Pipeline Board

Kanban columns by status

Lifecycle as four columns — Eligible → Submitted → Approved → Denied — with compact cards for each (agent × carrier). Most visual; you instantly see where things pile up.

Best for: seeing workflow bottlenecks at a glance.
Trade-off: cards-over-tables fights the app's dense table style; data truncates.
Ethos fit 2/5 · covers all 3 needs (alerts strongest)

B · Coverage Matrix

Agents × carriers grid

The densest view: every downline agent is a row, every carrier a column, each cell a status chip. Per-carrier totals across the top, your own row pinned with “newly eligible” chips.

Best for: max information density, portfolio-at-a-glance.
Trade-off: gets unwieldy past ~15–20 carriers; weak on small screens.
Ethos fit 4/5 · densest of the three

C · Roster + Action Inbox RECOMMENDED

Master-detail + always-on Action Center

An always-visible Action Center (alerts + the approvals inbox) sits on top; below, a downline roster drives a per-agent carrier detail table with inline editing. A segmented control flips to “My Contracting”.

Best for: how uplines actually work — pick an agent, drill in.
Why recommended: the only one that fully covers all three needs and stays table-first.
