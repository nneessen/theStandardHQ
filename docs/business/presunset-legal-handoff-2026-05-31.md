## PRIORITY ITEM 1 — IP ownership of the platform (Clause E)

This is the single biggest exposure: whether Founders can claim ownership of, or rights in, The
Standard HQ platform through the Agreement's IP-assignment clause.

### Clause E — verbatim (as transcribed from the executed Agreement)

> **E.** Any information, know-how, data, results, and inventions, and any associated intellectual
> property, that is made, discovered, created, invented, or generated by Contractor, its members,
> managers, agents, or affiliates **in connection with the Services, or the Company**, shall be
> owned by Company and Contractor hereby assigns all rights in and to the same to the Company.

### Engineering facts the owner can prove

- The platform was **substantially built before the Agreement.** Git history shows **1,521 commits
  across 199 distinct active days** between **2025-09-24** and the Agreement date **2026-05-11** —
  ~7.5 months of continuous, finished-product development. First commit 2025-09-24 18:15:31; product
  code (`src/features/`) from 2025-09-26. Full record + reproduction commands + the third-party
  corroboration checklist (domain WHOIS, Supabase/Vercel/Stripe creation dates) are in
  `platform-provenance-evidence-2026-05-31.md`.
- The owner operates the platform as an **independent commercial product** (multi-tenant: Founders,
  Self Made, and the unrelated Epic Life tenant), not as a deliverable created for the Company.

### The position for counsel to evaluate (NOT a conclusion)

On its face and read in isolation, Clause E appears **scoped and forward-looking**: it assigns IP
"made... created... generated... **in connection with the Services, or the Company**." It contains
**no express assignment of pre-existing IP** and **no retroactive language**. The argument to be
weighed is that a platform created independently, ~7.5 months **before** the Agreement (and before
any "Services" existed between the parties), is **pre-existing IP outside Clause E's scope**.

### Questions for counsel

1. Does Clause E, read with the Agreement's **definition of "Services"** and any other IP provisions,
   reach **pre-existing** IP — or only IP created after/under the Agreement?
2. The phrase **"or the Company"** — could a court read "in connection with... the Company" broadly
   enough to capture the platform merely because Founders agents *used* it? How does the
   creation-timing (pre-Agreement) bear on that?
3. **Post-signing development** (commits after 2026-05-11, ongoing) — is later work on a pre-existing
   product separable from the protected pre-existing core? How should the owner treat continued
   development to preserve the position?
4. Given the Agreement is already signed, is it advisable/possible to obtain an **addendum or side
   letter** expressly confirming The Standard HQ as the owner's pre-existing property, excluded from
   Clause E (a "Prior Inventions" carve-out after the fact)? What leverage/risk does requesting one create?
5. Does signing an Agreement containing Clause E and then moving to cut off Founders weeks later
   create any **estoppel, waiver, or sequencing** problem for the IP position?

## ITEM 2 — Platform Terms of Service adequacy (for an adversarial shutoff)

- **Facts:** Public Terms (`src/.../TermsPage.tsx`) were hardened with anti-IMO clauses + Colorado
  governing law; the privacy/Terms are presented to users. The platform can technically discontinue
  access per-tenant.
- **Questions for counsel:** Do the Terms give the owner an explicit, enforceable right to
  **discontinue service** to a tenant, with no uptime/availability guarantee, no warranty, and a
  limitation of liability — sufficient to withstand an **adversarial** shutoff of Founders? Do the
  Terms bind the agents who used the platform (acceptance/records)? Is there a conflict between the
  Terms (owner's document) and the Agreement (Founders' document)?

## ITEM 3 — Data-wipe legality

- **Facts:** The sunset design is a **two-switch** system: a **reversible** access flag
  (`imos.access_revoked_at`) and a separate **irreversible** data wipe. An **export-first** flow
  generates a full data export bundle for every affected user before any wipe (engineering parity
  verification — that the export covers everything the wipe deletes — is Task 2A.1, in progress).
- **Questions for counsel:** Does the owner have the right to **delete** Founders agents' business
  data? Are there **insurance / records-retention** obligations (carrier, state DOI, NAIC) that
  require retaining certain records regardless? Does the export-first sequence adequately mitigate a
  "you destroyed my business records" claim, and should there be a notice + retrieval window before
  any deletion?

## ITEM 4 — Tortious interference / business disruption

- **Facts:** Cutting agents off mid-business could be framed as interference with their books/income.
  The platform supports a **notice period** and **wind-down** if counsel advises one.
- **Questions for counsel:** What **notice period** and **wind-down terms** (if any) are needed? Is a
  **documented, legitimate business rationale** (not retaliation) required, and what should it
  contain? How does Clause G (non-replacement) interact with cutting off the tooling agents use?

## ITEM 5 — Entity shield + insurance (risk transfer)

- **Facts:** The owner currently holds the platform IP and operates it **personally** → direct
  personal liability. No LLC/insurance confirmed in-repo.
- **Questions for counsel / broker:** Should the IP be assigned to an **LLC/Corp** (with proper
  formalities) before any adversarial action? Are **cyber-liability + tech E&O + general liability**
  policies in place and adequate? Does forming an entity now (post-signing) affect the IP position?
